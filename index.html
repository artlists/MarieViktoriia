who controls codependency? and why emotions are so hidden from the one who's experiencing them?
PERFORMED BY @babemariaj and @vulgarways
CREATED BY @mpalamarcuk and @milo2001.xyz
In this series, visual artist Marie Viktoriia [@babemarieviktoriia] and photographer [@burjmak] stage a quiet confrontation between desire, control, and sanctity. Shot in the intimacy of a hotel room, the images draw on religious iconography and fetish aesthetics to question how vulnerability and power coexist within the same frame. Lace, fur, and restraint become visual metaphors, not merely for seduction, but for the ways femininity is read, performed, and consumed. Viktoriia's gaze refuses simplification; each gesture oscillates between offering and defiance, between obedience and domination.

Marie Viktoriia is a performance artist and visual creator working across live performance, video, and conceptual image-making. Born in Russia, she works internationally across independent art projects and visual collaborations.

Her practice explores the body as both medium and interface â€” a site where intimacy, surveillance, technology, and vulnerability intersect. Through minimal gestures, durational actions, and carefully constructed visual situations, Viktoriia creates performances that move between tension and stillness, control and exposure.

Defined by stark compositions and a psychologically charged visual language, her projects examine how identity is performed under constant observation and how private experience shifts when staged or mediated through screens.

Alongside her artistic practice, she collaborates on visual and commercial projects, bringing a performance-based approach to fashion, music, and editorial imagery. She works across Asia and worldwide.
